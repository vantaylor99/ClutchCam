Reconnect acceptance now uses data-plane advancement as the primary
machine-verifiable criterion. FFmpeg child exit/start logs remain useful
diagnostics when the child actually exits or restarts, but they are no longer
required for acceptance when SRS keeps the RTMP consumer process alive across
bounded publishers.

What changed:

- `compose_generated_ingest_checkpoint.py` supports `--reconnect-proof`,
  `--reconnect-gap-seconds`, `GENERATED_INGEST_RECONNECT_PROOF`, and
  `GENERATED_INGEST_RECONNECT_GAP_SECONDS`.
- The checkpoint report always includes a `reconnect` section.
- Reconnect proof performs a second bounded publish after the first ready
  buffer and requires every requested stream's latest segment sequence to
  advance.
- The proof compares `buffer-worker` Compose service identity before and after
  the second publish, preferring container ID when available.
- The Linux runbook documents sequence advancement as the acceptance gate and
  treats FFmpeg exit/start logs as diagnostics.
